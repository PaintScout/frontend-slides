The big idea

Composition is destiny

The big idea:

What a planet is made of does not just fill it in. It decides everything. The surface, the color, the weather, even whether there is any solid ground to stand on.

Rock is rare
Only 4 of 8 are made of rock.

The other four are mostly gas and ice. In our solar system, solid ground is the exception, not the rule.

The Rocky Worlds

Mercury, Venus, Earth and Mars. Built from silicate rock and metal, with surfaces forged by lava, impacts and rust.

Mercury

An iron heart in a thin stone shell.

Mercury is mostly metal. A giant iron core fills about 85% of its radius, wrapped in a thin, ancient crust of silicate rock.

  • Iron core takes up roughly 85% of the planet
  • Crust of basalt and pale anorthosite rock
  • Heavily cratered surface, billions of years old

Venus

A whole planet paved in basalt.

Venus is wrapped almost entirely in volcanic rock. Beneath a crushing carbon-dioxide sky, basalt plains and shield volcanoes cover most of the surface.

  • Over 80% of the surface is volcanic basalt
  • Hundreds of shield volcanoes, some likely active
  • Surface near 465°C, hot enough to melt lead

Earth
Earth is the only world with granite.

Continents are granite, the ocean floor is basalt. No other planet we know of makes both. It is the fingerprint of plate tectonics and liquid water working together.

Mars

Basalt that has literally rusted.

Mars is built from basalt, like Venus, but its iron-rich dust has oxidized over billions of years. That rust is what makes the planet red.

  • Basaltic crust under a coat of iron-oxide dust
  • Olympus Mons, a basalt volcano about 22 km tall
  • Dry riverbeds hint at a warmer, wetter past

02

The Gas Giants

Jupiter and Saturn. Vast worlds of hydrogen and helium with no solid surface to land on, hiding a rocky core far below.

Jupiter & Saturn

There is no surface to stand on.

Dive into a gas giant and you would never hit ground. Gas just thickens into liquid, then into something stranger.

01 Gas & liquid — mostly hydrogen and helium, the same stuff as the Sun.
02 Metallic hydrogen — crushing pressure turns hydrogen into a liquid metal deep inside.
03 A buried core — rock and ice worth perhaps 10 to 20 Earths sits at the center.

The Ice Giants

Uranus and Neptune. Worlds of water, ammonia and methane ice over a rocky core, painted blue by the methane in their skies.

Uranus & Neptune

Not gas, not rock, but ice.

Astronomers call them ices: water, ammonia and methane squeezed into a hot, slushy mantle around a rocky core. Methane in the air absorbs red light and turns them blue.

  • Mantle of hot water, ammonia and methane ice
  • Rock-and-iron core roughly the mass of Earth
  • Neptune has the fastest winds in the solar system
