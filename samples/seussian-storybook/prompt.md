# Sample Prompt — Original Seuss-Style Children's Storybook

## What a user would type

> Make me a fun children's storybook presentation — one page per slide — written
> in a bouncy, rhyming, made-up-words style reminiscent of classic whimsical
> kids' books. IMPORTANT: it has to be an ORIGINAL story with original characters
> and original invented words — do not copy or reference Dr. Seuss characters,
> titles, or text (no copyright trouble). Just the same playful *spirit*: anapestic
> rhyme, silly creatures, a gentle little lesson at the end. Bright, hand-drawn,
> joyful look. Each page should rhyme and feel like a page-turn.

## Generation notes (for fine-tuning comparison)

- **Mode:** A — New Presentation, from scratch (no images; whimsical CSS-only
  shapes/creatures built from gradients, border-radius blobs, and SVG).
- **Length:** Short/Medium — target 10–12 pages including a title and a closing
  "the end" page.
- **Original story:** Invent everything. Suggested: a roly-poly creature called a
  **Wibblewog named Bo** who has lost his **Zibbentine Zatch** and bumbles across
  invented places (the Snorkle Marsh, Mount Fluvial) meeting invented critters,
  learning that the thing he lost was with him all along / that asking for help
  is brave. Original invented words throughout (zatch, gnorf, wibble, snurmple).
- **Style chosen:** Bright, playful, hand-drawn whimsy — think construction-paper
  collage. Warm cream paper background, fat rounded primary-color shapes, a
  rounded friendly display font (e.g. Fredoka / Baloo style) plus a readable
  rounded body face. Bouncy, springy reveal animations (gentle wobble/pop), big
  page numbers, page-turn feel. NOT a dark or corporate theme.
- **Rhyme:** Every page is a short rhyming stanza (AABB or ABCB), 2–4 lines,
  large and centered so it reads like a picture book.
- **Inline editing:** No.
