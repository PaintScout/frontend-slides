A bright little tale of a roly-poly fellow

Bo the
Wibblewog

& the Lost Zibbentine Zatch

In a snug little snuggery, round as a log,
lived a roly-poly Wibblewog — his name, it was Bo.
He was happy and bouncy from his toes to his top,
with a hop and a wibble and a wibbly-wog wop!

2

Now Bo had one treasure he loved more than much:
a glittery, glimmery Zibbentine Zatch.
But he woke up one morning and let out a YELP —
his Zatch wasn't there! Oh, who, oh who'd help?

3

He bumbled and rumbled to the Snorkle Marsh bog,
where the mud goes ker-splatch and the frogs go ker-glog.
“Have you seen my dear Zatch?” called poor little Bo —
but the marsh only burbled a bubbly “no.”

4

Then a Snurmple popped up with a curious blink:
“You look mighty mopey. Now, what do you think?
A Zatch in the marsh? That's a hard thing to catch —
have you peeked, my dear Wibblewog, under your patch?”

5

So he climbed, oh he climbed, up Mount Fluvial's height,
past the gloop and the goop and the gniff out of sight.
“My Zatch! Is it here, in the snow and the scratch?”
But the mountain just shrugged. There was no sign of Zatch.

6

In the deep, drowsy Gnorfwood he met, with a start,
a Quibbling Gnorf with a grumble-y heart.
“You want HELP?” harrumphed Gnorf. “Well, it's perfectly true —
the bravest of beasties just ask when they're blue.”

7

So Bo took a breath and he called, loud and clear,
“I'm stuck, and I'm sad, and I need a friend here!”
And the marsh-folk and gnorf-folk all came in a batch,
every snurmple and snorkle, to hunt for his Zatch.

8

Then the Snurmple peeked close at Bo's wibbly back —
“Why, you silly old softie… that's where is your Zatch!”
It had ridden along, snuggled up in his fur,
the whole bumbly way, in a glittery whirr!

9

Oh, Bo did a jig and a jubilant JOMP,
with a wibble, a wobble, a thankity-stomp!
“You looked, and you helped, and you stuck by my side —
and the asking,” smiled Bo, “was the bravest I've tried.”

10

So if ever you're lost, or you've mislaid your match,
or your sock, or your smile, or your own little Zatch —
just ask, and be brave, and you'll surely soon see:
the best things were close as close-can-be to thee.

11

The End

…and Bo wibbled happily ever after.
